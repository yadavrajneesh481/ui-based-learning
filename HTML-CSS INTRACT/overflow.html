What is CSS Overflow?

Overflow controls what happens when content is too big to fit inside its container!

üåä overflow: visible

This is way too much text for this small container! It will spill out and be visible outside the borders. This is the default behavior.
overflow: visible;
Default behavior: Content spills outside the container and remains visible.

This is way too much text for this small container! But now it gets cut off and hidden. You can't see the rest of this text because it's clipped.
overflow: hidden;
Clips content: Extra content is cut off and completely hidden from view.

üìú overflow: scroll

This is way too much text for this small container! But now you can scroll to see all the content. Scrollbars are always visible, even when not needed.
overflow: scroll;
Always scrollable: Scrollbars are always present, regardless of content size.

ü§ñ overflow: auto

This is way too much text for this small container! Scrollbars appear automatically only when needed. This is usually the best choice for most situations.
overflow: auto;
Smart scrolling: Scrollbars only appear when content actually overflows.

üí° Quick Tips

overflow: auto is usually the best choice for most situations

overflow: scroll when you always want scrollbars visible
